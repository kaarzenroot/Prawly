"Communication is getting faster, but it isn’t getting any kinder. "

While exploring anonymous tools, I noticed a recurring flaw: the "Anonymity-Toxicity Loop." When names are removed, empathy often vanishes. Valuable feedback gets lost in the noise, and the psychological cost is far too high.

I’m building Prawly to bridge that gap. 🚀

The Solution 💡
Prawly is an AI-driven "empathy layer." Using a stateless architecture, it performs real-time Radio Edits on messages—stripping the sting while preserving the constructive intent.
Core Use Cases 🛠️

      • Institutional Feedback: Students can speak hard truths without fear, while leadership receives usable data without toxic delivery.

      • The "Inner Circle" Mirror: A safe space for teams to share the genuine critiques usually avoided to "save feelings."

       • Social & Fun: A safer, smarter alternative to NGL. Enjoy the fun of anonymous interaction without the anxiety of unchecked toxicity.

The Vision 🎯
I am also developing "Org-Mode"—Prawly’s corporate twin. It is a dedicated system for organizations to turn high-friction communication into high-utility data, prioritizing psychological safety and institutional growth.
